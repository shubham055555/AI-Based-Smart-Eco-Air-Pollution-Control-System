Problem Description
Air pollution has become a serious environmental and health concern across the world, affecting both urban and semi-urban regions. A large portion of air pollution is generated from specific high-emission points such as industrial chimneys, enclosed indoor spaces, commercial areas, and locations with limited ventilation. Once pollutants are released, they quickly spread into the surrounding environment, making pollution control more difficult and less effective.

Most existing air purification solutions focus on improving air quality after pollution has already dispersed over a wide area. These systems are primarily designed for indoor environments, are expensive to install and maintain, and rely on synthetic or non-biodegradable filters. As a result, they are not suitable for deployment at pollution source locations or for outdoor use. Moreover, many solutions are not affordable enough for large-scale adoption, especially in developing regions.

Another major limitation of current systems is the lack of localized pollution control. Instead of targeting the origin of pollution, conventional approaches attempt to clean ambient air, which requires higher energy consumption and delivers limited impact. This reactive approach fails to prevent the spread of harmful pollutants and does not provide a scalable solution for controlling air pollution effectively.

Additionally, the absence of intelligent decision-making in traditional systems reduces their efficiency. Without real-time analysis and adaptive response mechanisms, these systems cannot adjust purification intensity based on changing pollution levels or environmental conditions.

Therefore, there is a critical need for an affordable, eco-friendly, and intelligent air pollution control solution that can be installed directly at pollution-intensive source locations. Such a system should be capable of operating in both indoor and outdoor environments, actively reducing pollution before it spreads. By using sustainable materials such as bamboo and charcoal, the solution can minimize environmental impact while remaining cost-effective and accessible for widespread deployment.
Objectives
The main objectives of this project are:

To reduce air pollution at its source by installing the system directly at high-emission locations, preventing the spread of pollutants into the surrounding environment.

To design an affordable air pollution control solution using eco-friendly materials such as bamboo and charcoal, making it accessible for large-scale deployment.

To enable both indoor and outdoor usage of the system so that it can be applied in various pollution-prone environments.

To continuously monitor air quality in real time using air quality sensors for detecting particulate matter and harmful gases.

To automatically activate air purification when pollution levels exceed safe limits, ensuring efficient and timely pollution control.

To integrate intelligent data analysis (AI-based decision-making) to identify pollution patterns and optimize purification performance.

To minimize environmental impact by reducing dependence on synthetic filters and promoting sustainable purification methods.

To develop a scalable and adaptable solution that can be easily customized for different pollution levels and deployment locations.
Proposed Solution
The proposed solution is the development of a smart, AI-enabled, eco-friendly air pollution control system designed to be installed directly at high pollution source locations to minimize the spread of pollutants. Unlike conventional air purifiers that operate only after pollution has dispersed, this system focuses on source-level pollution control, making the purification process more effective and energy-efficient.

The system continuously monitors air quality using multiple sensors to measure parameters such as particulate matter and harmful gases. These sensors collect real-time environmental data, which is processed by an Arduino-based embedded system. Using AI-based data analysis, the system identifies pollution patterns, evaluates pollution severity, and determines the optimal purification response.

When pollution levels exceed predefined or intelligently predicted thresholds, the system automatically activates an eco-friendly purification unit. This purification unit is constructed using natural materials such as bamboo and charcoal, which act as effective natural filters by absorbing pollutants and reducing harmful particles in the air. A controlled airflow mechanism ensures that polluted air passes through the purification media before being released back into the environment.

The system is designed to support both indoor and outdoor environments, making it suitable for deployment in industrial zones, workshops, public spaces, classrooms, and other pollution-prone areas. Its low-cost design and use of sustainable materials ensure affordability, scalability, and minimal environmental impact.

By integrating real-time sensing, intelligent decision-making, and sustainable purification, the proposed solution bridges the gap between passive air quality monitoring and active pollution control. The system not only improves air quality locally but also contributes to long-term environmental sustainability by preventing pollution at its source.
Technology Stack
Arduino UNO, Air Quality Sensors (PM2.5, MQ135, DHT11), AI-based decision logic, Embedded C, Bamboo & Charcoal filtration system, DC motor, I2C LCD.
